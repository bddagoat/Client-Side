About The MOCC

My name is Brandon L. Davis and I am the director of this website/catalogue. I am an alumni of Hampden-Sydney College. The motivation behind this platform came from the education I continued to research after gaining my Computer Science degree. At an early age, I educated myself about the history of my ancestory and what comes with being an African American in America. As I expand my knowledge, I realized that my community along with various others that resembled mine do not have all of the proper resources to reach their full potential. In addition to this, their businesses were not doing as well as others. During the early 1900s, the potential of Tulsa, the Hayti community in Durham, Jackson Ward in Richmond, along with others was cut short due to hate and violence. The minority market hasn't had a similar standing since those communities existed. My goal is to restore what these places were determined to accomplish which was the inclusion of a creative educational and artistic environment and increasing the overall value of the small business market. This inclusive environment will be designed to be a digitalized interface where talented artists and business owners can promote their content.
This website is a unique project that will serve as a platform where foremost underprivileged businessowners can promote their services and goods for our community. The Creator's Catalogue also displays a galore of minority art/media. It is no longer a secret that since the beginning of this country there has been animosity towards people who aren't White Americans and our advancement. MOCC will be a non-discrimatory environment where ANY minority group or person of interest can safely advertise display their brand/art. The main goal of this website is to promote minority creativity and help centralize the investments of minority communities. We must keep the returns of the investments inside of the community so the youth will benefit from an ample size of resources provided to them. This is just the beginning, THE MARATHON CONTINUES!
